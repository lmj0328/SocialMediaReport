Our Project

Our project utilizes an interactive website for users to explore their usage patterns and self-identities on multiple social media platforms helping users self-reflect as well as self-track of their social media usage.

The Problem

According to our online research and literature review, people are spending an increasing amount of hours daily on a wide range of social media; however, a considerable number of them do not have enough time to reflect on their postings. As people post interesting facts about themselves and view contents about others, their self-presentation and identity may be gradually changed by the affordances without their noticing. This lack of self-awareness is detrimental to people as it causes anxiety and confusion. In addition, different types of users can be curious about what they have posted or browsed in order to have a more purposeful and conscious social life and or achieve a work-life balance.

On the other hand, based on our marketing research and user research (a survey with 80 responses mainly from college students), over 70% of participants stated that they use the social media platform over 3 hours every day and around 90% participants show their interest to know about their usage pattern. The marketing research shows that the music app called NetEase Cloud Music has a yearly report that displays information like how many hours a user has spent on this app over the past year, which indicates that users care about this type of information and there is a potential trend of it getting popular.
